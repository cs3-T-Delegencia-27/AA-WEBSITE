MEDICAL TECHNOLOGY AS A PRE-MED COURSE
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN MEDICAL TECHNOLOGY
When pursuing a career as a doctor or physician, BS Medical Technology
is an excellent pre-medical education. In general, a bachelor's degree in medical
technology is a four-year curriculum that includes both general education and
professional courses. The fourth year is a one-year internship program in a
Commission on Higher Education (CHED)-accredited training laboratory, with
rotational duties in
1. Clinical Chemistry
2. Hematology
3. Microbiology
4. Immunohematology (Blood Banking)
5. Immunology and Serology
6. Urinalysis and other Body Fluids (Clinical Microscopy)
7. Parasitology
8. Histopathologic/Cytological Techniques
9. and other emerging technologies.

THE ROLE OF UNDERGRADUATE INTERNSHIPS AND JOB TRAINING PROGRAMS
Experience in medical and healthcare programs offers great contributions to enhancing one's skills and capabilities. Books are of great assistance but when interactions and raw participation can give much in-depth meaning compared to superficial information presented just by a book. These student internships, according to successful doctors and physicians, developed their sense of critical thinking, making them better prepared to tackle emergencies in real-time conditions.
Furthermore, the path to becoming a Medical Technologist entails perseverance in completing several residency programs and trainings that can help with future board tests preparation. Although the subjects addressed in this experience are difficult, such as anatomy, pharmacology, and pediatrics, it provides a solid basis to obtain a better grasp of your studies because it considers the exact familiarization of functions and systems.
